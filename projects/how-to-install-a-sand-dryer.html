methods for sand drying

Methods for mining and preparing sand vary slightly based on deposit location and quality of deposit. However, a typical sand flow sheet consists of two basic portions- wet and dry.

WET FLOW: The wet flow is designed to both remove clay slimes and break up any agglomerates. It can also function as a preliminary sizing step in removing excess fines. Washing is the simplest and lowest cost method for cleaning sand.

DRY FLOW: The dry flow is designed to remove moisture in the sand and size the sand into the various sand products. Typical Sand sizes are 12/20, 20/40, 40/70 and 70/140 mesh.

sand dryer installation

The new sand and aggregate dryer operates at just one “G” of vibrational force. This is the minimum required to ensure a dry, uniform product at the discharge, and energy-efficient fluidization. The drive mechanism allows full adjustment of important parameters, such as amplitude, frequency, and angle of vibration. The entire machine is dynamically isolated to reduce the reaction to the supporting foundation. Insulation is attached with a steel skin and hot air is supplied to the machine through a flexible metal “bellow” in lieu of fabric flexible connectors. The result is the most reliable fluid bed available today.
